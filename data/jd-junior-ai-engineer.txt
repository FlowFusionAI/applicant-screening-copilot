JUNIOR AI ENGINEER
Brightvale Labs — Austin, TX (hybrid, 3 days on-site) | Full-time
Posted: June 2026 | Req #2026-114

ABOUT BRIGHTVALE
Brightvale Labs builds AI-assisted workflow tools for mid-market logistics and
healthcare companies. We're a 40-person, Series A company. Our products turn
messy operational documents — invoices, intake forms, dispatch logs — into
structured, auditable decisions.

THE ROLE
You'll join a six-person product engineering team and build LLM-powered
features end to end: prompt design, structured outputs, retrieval pipelines,
and the automated evals that keep all of it honest. This is a hands-on
engineering role for someone early in their career who has already shipped
something real with LLM APIs — professionally, in an internship, or as a
serious side project.

WHAT YOU'LL DO
- Design and iterate on prompts and structured-output schemas for extraction,
  classification, and scoring features
- Build retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines: chunking, embeddings,
  vector search, reranking
- Write and maintain automated evals; catch quality regressions before
  customers do
- Ship TypeScript and Python services that wrap LLM APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic)
  behind clean, tested interfaces
- Instrument token usage, latency, and cost; propose and validate cheaper
  model routings
- Participate in code review and pair regularly with senior engineers

WHAT WE'RE LOOKING FOR
- 0–2 years of professional software engineering experience (internships and
  substantial personal projects count)
- Strong Python or TypeScript; comfort with both is a plus
- Hands-on experience calling LLM APIs and handling their failure modes:
  hallucination, format drift, prompt injection
- Familiarity with embeddings and vector stores (pgvector, Pinecone,
  Weaviate, or similar)
- A testing mindset: you don't ship prompt changes without a way to measure
  whether they helped
- Working knowledge of Git, CI, and at least one cloud platform

NICE TO HAVE
- Vercel AI SDK, LangChain, or LlamaIndex in a real project
- Zod or Pydantic for schema validation
- Fine-tuning experience (LoRA/QLoRA) or eval frameworks (promptfoo,
  Braintrust)
- Open-source contributions or shipped side projects we can look at
- SQL and basic data modeling

annual learning budget, relocation assistance available.
